Revenue Share Structures

Industry benchmarks from creator platforms, game publishers, and SaaS partnerships inform our recommended approach.

Platform / Model Creator/Developer Share Platform/Partner Share Notes

Substack 90% 10% Newsletter platform; minimal support
Indie Game Publishers 70% 30% Marketing, QA, publishing services
YouTube 55% 45% Full platform + distribution + monetization

Moonshot (Recommended) 60% (pilot) ‚Üí 50% 40% (pilot) ‚Üí 50% Full app rewrite + monetization + ops
Recommendation
Start with 60/40 (developer-favored) for first 3-5 partnerships to attract early adopters and build track record. Move to 50/50 once value proposition is proven. For high-potential apps (500K+ users), consider 70/30 to win competitive deals.

Net Revenue Definition

What's Included
  • In-app purchases (consumables & non-consumables)
  • Subscription revenue (monthly/annual)
  • Ad revenue (AdMob, FAN, etc.)
  • Paid app downloads
Deductions Before Split
  • Platform fees (Apple/Google 15-30%)
  • Refunds and chargebacks
  • Payment processing fees
  • Taxes collected (sales tax, VAT)
Critical Clause
Revenue share should be calculated on "proceeds actually received" by the operating entity, not on gross sales reported by app stores. This protects both parties from disputes over money never collected.

IP & Ownership Framework

A dual-ownership model protects both parties while enabling Moonshot to build reusable infrastructure across partnerships.

Developer Owns

  • Original app concept & design
  • Original codebase (if any)
  • App name & trademark
  • Brand assets & identity
  • User data & relationships

Moonshot Owns

  • Newly written code (rewrite)
  • Shared infrastructure & SDKs
  • Monetization implementations
  • Analytics integrations
  • Operational playbooks

License Structure

Developer Gets
Exclusive Perpetual License
Developer receives an exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use Moonshot's code for their app. This ensures the developer can always operate their app, even if the partnership ends.
Moonshot Gets
Non-Exclusive Reuse License
Moonshot receives a non-exclusive license to reuse generic/framework code components in other partnerships (not app-specific logic). This enables building scalable shared infrastructure.

App Store Account Strategy

Recommended Approach
App stays in developer's account. Moonshot added as Admin/App Manager + Sales role. Developer remits Moonshot's share monthly. This preserves developer control and reduces legal complexity while giving Moonshot full operational access.
Platform Transfer Possible? Shared Access Roles Recommendation
Apple App Store Yes, with restrictions Admin, App Manager, Developer, Marketing, Sales Add Moonshot as App Manager + Sales
Google Play Yes, straightforward Owner, Admin, App Manager, Developer, Finances Add Moonshot as Admin + Finances
Revenue Verification
Use third-party analytics (AppFigures, RevenueCat) that both parties can access. This builds trust through transparency and avoids disputes over self-reported revenue figures.

Step 1: 30-day informal negotiation
Step 2: Non-binding mediation ($5-10K)

Mutual: Each party indemnifies for their breaches
IP-specific: Developer indemnifies for original app IP; Moonshot for new code IP

Representations & Warranties

Developer Represents
  • Owns all rights to original app and can enter agreement
  • App doesn't infringe third-party IP
  • Has rights to use all third-party libraries/APIs
  • No existing agreements prevent this partnership
Moonshot Represents
  • Will write professional-quality code following industry standards
  • New code won't infringe third-party IP
  • Will maintain app security and comply with store guidelines
  • Has expertise to execute monetization strategy
