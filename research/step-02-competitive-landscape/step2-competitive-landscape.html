White Space Opportunities

Four key differentiators that define Moonshot's unique position.

1. Vertical Gap: Non-Gaming Mobile

Gaming publishing is mature with established players. Utility, education, lifestyle, and productivity apps have no equivalent support infrastructure.

Evidence: Tilting Point's 2025 pivot validates this. Non-gaming apps now exceed gaming in total app store revenue.

2. Model Gap: Partnership vs Acquisition

All existing players are acquirers. Even "publishers" take significant control. Developers want to keep ownership.

Moonshot: True partnership where developer keeps meaningful ownership while getting operational lift.

3. Service Gap: Tech + Monetization

Gaming publishers provide both but only for games. Holding companies provide neither. E-commerce aggregators focus on ops only.

Moonshot: Full-stack service (technical rebuild + monetization optimization) for non-gaming apps.

4. Developer Experience Gap

Gaming publishers optimize for volume (1 in 150 acceptance). Focus on $0.50-$1.50 LTV hyper-casual. Treat developers as content suppliers.

Moonshot: Developer-centric partnership with focus on sustainable subscription/IAP revenue.

Competitive Threats

Risks to monitor and how to mitigate them.

Tilting Point Expansion

Threat Level: High

First mover in non-gaming app investment with $150M fund and established relationships.

Mitigation: They offer UA funding only, not operational partnership. Our differentiation: full technical + monetization support.

Gaming Publishers Entering Non-Gaming

Threat Level: Medium

If CrazyLabs or Voodoo decide to expand beyond gaming, they have resources and expertise.

Mitigation: Their DNA is hyper-casual gaming. Different skill set required for subscription/utility apps. Build portfolio and playbook early.

Strategic Recommendations

Actionable steps based on competitive analysis.

1. Reposition Against Tilting Point

Action: Update messaging to explicitly contrast with UA-only models.

Positioning: "UA funding gives you fuel. Moonshot builds the engine, optimizes the route, and drives the car."

Target: Developers with technical debt, poor analytics, or unclear monetization strategy who won't benefit from UA funding alone.

2. Move Fast on Pilot

Urgency: HIGH - Tilting Point's December 2025 deals show they're actively deploying capital.

Goal: Demonstrate full partnership model before Tilting Point scales to 10+ apps.

3. Monitor Tilting Point's Portfolio

Action: Track Clean Manager and lifestyle app partner metrics via Sensor Tower, data.ai.

Why: If UA-only approach shows limitations (retention issues, poor monetization), it validates Moonshot's holistic approach.

4. Partner with UA Funders

Strategic Play: Position Moonshot as the operational layer for UA-funded apps. "You got the money - we'll build the engine."

This turns Tilting Point from competitor to lead generation source.

5. Target Apps Rejected by UA Funders

Insight: UA funders require high ROAS and strong retention. Many good apps won't meet their bar.

Moonshot's Advantage: We can fix technical issues and improve monetization first, making apps UA-ready later.

Key Takeaways

What this means for Moonshot.

Market Timing is Favorable

Non-gaming apps now larger than gaming by revenue. Users spend 90% of mobile time in non-gaming categories. Tilting Point's pivot validates the opportunity.

Gaming Playbook Exists

Supersonic and CrazyLabs have proven the partnership publishing model works. We can adapt their approach (testing, scaling, rev share) for non-gaming apps.

Acquisition Models Dominate

The partnership model is genuinely differentiated. Developers who want to keep ownership have no current option for operational support.

No Direct Competitor

Partnership + operational lift for non-gaming indie mobile apps is an unoccupied market position. First-mover advantage is available.
