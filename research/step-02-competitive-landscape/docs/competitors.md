# Competitive Landscape

## Executive Summary

The mobile app partnership/acquisition space is dominated by **gaming-focused players**. Major consolidators (AppLovin, Supersonic, Voodoo, CrazyLabs) exclusively target hyper-casual and casual gaming. Holding companies (Tiny) focus on SaaS/web businesses. E-commerce aggregators (OpenStore) target Shopify stores.

**Key Finding:** No established player targets non-gaming indie mobile apps with a partnership (vs. acquisition) model. This represents Moonshot's primary white space opportunity.

## Gaming Publishers (Hyper-Casual/Casual Focus)

### AppLovin
- **Current Model:** Pure-play AI-driven ad-tech platform (pivoted away from gaming)
- **Major Shift (2025):** Sold entire mobile gaming division for ~$900M to Tripledot
- **Revenue:** $1.4B in Q3 2025 (68% YoY growth)
- **Focus:** AXON 2.0 AI advertising platform, expanding to web/CTV/e-commerce
- **Developer Terms:** N/A (exited publishing)
- **Relevance to Moonshot:** Low - no longer in app partnerships

### IronSource (Now Unity)
- **Model:** SDK-based monetization + game publishing (merged with Unity 2022)
- **Focus:** Gaming analytics and ad mediation
- **Developer Terms:** SDK integration with revenue share through advertising
- **Relevance to Moonshot:** Low - gaming-only, SDK-focused

### Supersonic Studios (Unity subsidiary)
- **Model:** Rapid testing and scaling of hyper-casual games
- **Scale:** 6.6B+ total installs, 190M+ MAU, 130+ games published
- **Process:**
  1. Test marketability/retention with experts
  2. Iterate on core mechanics
  3. Optimize ad campaigns and scale
- **Developer Terms:** Milestone-based (case studies show 45% IAP revenue share)
- **Support:** Unity analytics, A/B testing, LiveOps, creative optimization
- **Relevance to Moonshot:** Low - gaming-only, requires high-volume downloads

### CrazyLabs
- **Model:** Publishing partnerships for hyper-casual games
- **Programs:**
  - **Standard Publishing:** High-bar KPI requirements (~1 in 150-200 games accepted)
  - **Publishing For All:** Lower-bar program for "B-tier" games that can still profit
    - Requirements: CPI â‰¤ $0.80, promising retention/ARPU
    - Developer keeps ownership and maintenance responsibility
    - Self-serve testing via CLIK Dashboard
- **Revenue Share:** 55% to developers (promotional challenge), standard terms undisclosed
- **Relevance to Moonshot:** Low - gaming-only, but "Publishing For All" model is similar concept

### Voodoo
- **Model:** High-volume hyper-casual game publishing
- **Revenue Split:** 70% from third-party games, 30% internal
- **Developer Payments:**
  - $20K at soft launch
  - $180K at confirmed release
  - ~$1M for games reaching 10M+ downloads
- **Monetization:** Ad-driven (LTV typically $0.50-$1.50), cross-promotion across portfolio
- **Focus:** Keeping CPI as low as possible, rapid prototyping
- **Relevance to Moonshot:** Low - gaming-only, ad-dependent model

## Non-Gaming App Players

### Tilting Point (NEW ENTRANT - 2025)
- **Model:** Non-dilutive UA funding for app growth
- **Major Pivot (2025):** Expanding from gaming-only to non-gaming apps
- **Investment:** $30M annually allocated to non-gaming
  - $12M to Quiet's Clean Manager (utility app)
  - $18M to undisclosed lifestyle app
- **Fund:** $150M total UA fund (launched March 2025)
- **Terms:** Non-dilutive - developers scale without giving up ownership
- **Rationale:** Non-gaming apps now exceed games in app store revenue; users spend 90% of mobile time in non-gaming categories
- **Relevance to Moonshot:** **HIGH - Direct competitor entering same space**
  - Difference: Tilting Point provides UA capital only, not operational partnership
  - Moonshot offers deeper operational support (rebuild, monetization optimization)

## Holding Companies / Acquirers

### Tiny (tiny.com)
- **Model:** Acquires and holds profitable internet businesses long-term
- **Portfolio:** 80+ companies, $250M+ revenue
- **Target Profile:**
  - $500K-$50M profit
  - 3+ years operating history
  - Unique advantage (brand, community, niche)
  - High-quality team
- **Deal Terms:**
  - Offer in 7 days, close in 30 days
  - $1M-$300M deal size
  - Cash upfront (simple structure)
  - Founders can stay or go
- **Focus:** SaaS, web products, design agencies (NOT mobile apps)
- **Management Style:** "Management by abdication" - minimal interference
- **Challenges:** Stock down 48%, margin compression, 27x net debt to operating cash flow
- **Relevance to Moonshot:** Low - different vertical (web/SaaS, not mobile apps)

### OpenStore
- **Model:** Acquires Shopify stores with automated valuation
- **Target Profile:**
  - $500K-$10M annual revenue
  - 6+ months operating history
  - 70%+ sales on Shopify
- **Terms:**
  - Offer in 24 hours, close in 1 week
  - 80% upfront, 20% after transition
- **Alternative - OpenStore Drive:**
  - 1-year managed operations
  - 10% management fee
  - OpenStore keeps excess profits above projections
- **Valuation:** $970M (Sept 2022)
- **Challenges:** Supply chain volatility, rising CAC, platform dependency
- **Relevance to Moonshot:** Low - e-commerce only, but Drive model is interesting hybrid

- **Key Value Drivers:**

  - Revenue diversification
  - Market position
  - Growth potential
- **Relevance to Moonshot:** Could be sourcing channel; provides valuation benchmarks

## White Space Opportunities for Moonshot

### 1. **Vertical Gap: Non-Gaming Mobile Apps**
The gaming publishing ecosystem is mature with established players. But utility, education, lifestyle, and productivity apps have **no equivalent support infrastructure**.

**Evidence:**
- Tilting Point's 2025 pivot validates market opportunity
- Non-gaming apps now exceed gaming in total app store revenue
- Users spend 90% of mobile time in non-gaming categories
- Gaming publishers explicitly exclude non-gaming apps

### 2. **Model Gap: Partnership vs. Acquisition**
All existing players are **acquirers** (even those with "publishing" models take significant control):
- Tiny, OpenStore: Full acquisition
- Supersonic, Voodoo: Effectively take control of game
- CrazyLabs "Publishing For All": Developer keeps ownership but low-value games only

**Moonshot Opportunity:** True partnership where developer keeps meaningful ownership and optional involvement, while getting operational lift.

### 3. **Service Gap: Technical + Monetization Combined**
- Gaming publishers: Focus on UA and ads, no technical rebuild
- Holding companies: No operational involvement
- E-commerce aggregators: Operations but no technical modernization
- Tilting Point (non-gaming): UA funding only, no technical/monetization support

**Moonshot Opportunity:** Full-stack service (technical rebuild + monetization optimization) that gaming publishers provide, but for non-gaming apps.

### 4. **Developer Experience Gap**
Gaming publishers optimize for volume and LTV:
- Accept 1 in 150-200 games
- Focus on hyper-casual with $0.50-$1.50 LTV
- Treat developers as content suppliers

**Moonshot Opportunity:** Developer-centric partnership with:
- Higher acceptance rate for qualifying apps
- Focus on sustainable subscription/IAP revenue (not just ads)
- Genuine partnership relationship

## Competitive Threats

### Immediate Threats
1. **Tilting Point's Expansion** - First mover in non-gaming app investment
   - Mitigation: They offer UA funding only, not operational partnership
   - Our differentiation: Full technical + monetization support

2. **Gaming Publishers Expanding** - If CrazyLabs/Voodoo decide to enter non-gaming
   - Mitigation: Their DNA is hyper-casual gaming; different skill set required
   - Our differentiation: Understanding of non-gaming monetization models

### Medium-Term Threats
3. **New Entrants** - Moonshot's model could be replicated
   - Mitigation: Build portfolio and playbook early
   - First-mover advantage in relationships

4. **Developer Self-Service** - Tools making monetization easier
   - Mitigation: Tools alone don't fix under-monetization; expertise required

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **No direct competitor** offers partnership + operational lift for non-gaming indie mobile apps
2. **Tilting Point is closest threat** but offers funding, not partnership
3. **Acquisition models dominate** - partnership model is genuinely differentiated
4. **Market timing is favorable** - non-gaming apps now larger than gaming by revenue
5. **Gaming playbook exists** - can adapt Supersonic/CrazyLabs partnership models for non-gaming
