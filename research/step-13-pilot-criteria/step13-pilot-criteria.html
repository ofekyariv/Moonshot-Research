Ideal First Partner Profile

Based on findings from Steps 4, 7, and 8, here's what makes the best pilot partner.

50K-150K

Monthly Active Users

<$1K/mo

Current Revenue

Under-monetized apps have room for 3-5x lift. Apps already earning $5K+/mo have less upside for dramatic improvement.

Tier 1

Technical Complexity

6-8 week implementation timeline. Simple CRUD, BaaS backend, single platform. Score 8-16 on the complexity scorecard.

Best Fit Persona: Burned Out Solo Dev

From Step 4 research, this persona is most likely to partner:

  • Wants "set it and forget it" passive income
  • Low control priority — willing to hand off operations
  • Fast decision timeline (2-4 weeks)
  • Key message: "We handle everything"

Target Categories

App types with proven monetization potential and low complexity:

  • Productivity (timers, focus apps, trackers)
  • Utilities (converters, calculators, tools)
  • Education (flashcards, study aids, quizzes)
  • Lifestyle (journals, habit trackers, planners)

Must-Haves vs Nice-to-Haves

Absolute requirements versus preferential criteria for pilot selection.

Must-Haves (Non-Negotiable)

  • MAU: 25K-150K (enough for meaningful results)
  • Current revenue: <$1K/month (clear upside)
  • D30 retention: >25% (proves product-market fit)
  • Technical complexity: Tier 1 or 2 (score 8-24)
  • Single platform focus (iOS OR Android)
  • Non-gaming category
  • Solo developer or team of 2 max
  • Not VC-backed or venture-funded
  • Willing to share full analytics access
  • Responds to outreach within 7 days
  • Available for weekly 30-min sync calls
  • Willing to sign 60-day pilot agreement

Nice-to-Haves (Preferential)

  • Warm introduction or existing relationship
  • iOS-primary (5.4x higher subscription ARPU)
  • App in top categories: productivity, utilities, education
  • Developer has technical blog/Twitter presence
  • App has 4.0+ star rating
  • App updated within last 90 days
  • Developer timezone overlap with US
  • Previous experience with analytics tools
  • Existing but underoptimized monetization
  • App has been live 6+ months
  • Developer has positive community reputation
  • App has growing (not declining) user base

Risk Assessment Framework

Identify, categorize, and mitigate risks before committing to a pilot.

LOW RISK
Proceed Confidently
  • Tier 1 technical complexity (8-16 score)
  • Burned Out Solo Dev persona
  • Warm introduction from trusted source
  • Full analytics access provided
  • Clear product-market fit (D30 >35%)
  • iOS-primary, US user base
  • Developer responds within 24 hours
MEDIUM RISK
Proceed with Caution
  • Tier 2 technical complexity (17-24 score)
  • Accidental Success or Emerging Market persona
  • Cold outreach (no warm intro)
  • Analytics access promised but not verified
  • Moderate retention (D30 25-35%)
  • Android-primary or mixed platform
  • Developer responds within 3-5 days
HIGH RISK
Avoid or Reject
  • Tier 3 technical complexity (25+ score)
  • Aspiring Entrepreneur persona (high control)
  • Refuses to share analytics
  • Unrealistic expectations (10x in 30 days)
  • Low retention (D30 <25%)
  • Both platforms required simultaneously
  • Developer slow to respond (>7 days)

Deal-Breakers (Automatic Rejection)

Technical

  • Real-time multiplayer
  • Video/photo editing
  • Offline-first with sync
  • HIPAA/PCI compliance

Business

  • Expensive third-party APIs
  • Ongoing content creation
  • UGC with moderation
  • Marketplace/e-commerce

Developer

  • Refuses analytics access
  • Wants upfront guarantee
  • Ghosts or misses calls
  • Negative community rep

Legal

  • IP/trademark issues
  • VC-backed or funded
  • Won't sign agreement
  • Direct competitor in portfolio

Pilot Terms Recommendation

Structured terms based on B2B pilot best practices and Step 4/10 findings.

Pilot 70/30 (dev keeps 70%) $500/month floor 60 days 30-day mutual no-fault
Full Partnership 50/50 None after proving value Ongoing 60-day notice
Scaled (6+ months) Tiered: 50%/40%/30% None Ongoing 90-day notice

60-Day Pilot Timeline

Days 0-14

Onboarding

Analytics audit, baseline metrics, integration setup, initial discoveries

Days 15-35

Implementation

Monetization improvements, A/B tests, paywall optimization, ad placement

Days 36-55

Optimization

Iterate on results, refine strategies, measure uplift, document learnings

Days 56-60

Decision

Final metrics review, go/no-go on full partnership, transition planning

Success Criteria (Convert to Full Partnership)

  • Revenue lift: 30%+ improvement from baseline
  • Implementation: 3+ monetization improvements shipped
  • Satisfaction: Developer NPS 8+ / 10
  • Engagement: Attended 80%+ of sync calls
  • Learning: 10+ documented insights

Exit Criteria (Friendly Termination)

  • Revenue lift: <15% improvement after 60 days
  • Technical blockers: Unforeseen complexity emerged
  • Misalignment: Goals or expectations diverged
  • Communication: Developer disengaged or unresponsive
  • Mutual agreement: Either party can exit with 30 days notice
