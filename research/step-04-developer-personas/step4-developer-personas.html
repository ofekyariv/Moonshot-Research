The Burned Out Solo Dev

"I just want my life back." Best Fit

Demographics

  • Age: 28-42 years old
  • Location: US, UK, Germany, tech hubs
  • Full-time engineer ($80-150K)
  • 5-10 years experience

Their App

  • 50K-300K MAU, productivity/utility
  • $0-500/month (under-monetized)
  • React Native, Flutter, or native
  • Updates slowing (every 2-3 months)

Say YES When

  • "Set it and forget it" promise
  • Minimal time (2 hrs/month max)
  • Partner handles ALL operations
  • Easy exit if it doesn't work

Say NO When

  • Required to do more dev work
  • IP transfer or ownership changes
  • Complex, long-term contracts
  • High-pressure sales tactics

"After intense cognitive work, finding energy for side projects is difficult... The transition from enjoyment to dread signals unsustainability."

The Accidental Success

"I built this for myself and somehow people love it." Good Fit

Demographics

  • Age: 25-38 years old
  • Location: Global, often non-US
  • Developer (employed or freelance)
  • Started as personal project

Their App

  • 15K-100K MAU, niche utilities
  • $0-200/month (usually free)
  • Flutter, Expo, or native
  • Sporadic updates based on interest

Say YES When

  • Proof of competence (case studies)
  • Respect for their creation and users
  • Transparent, simple terms
  • Low-risk trial period

Say NO When

  • Partner seems profit-motivated only
  • Aggressive monetization plans
  • IP ownership transfer
  • Condescending attitude

"I cannot find time to develop this more (and also market it honestly) so I've decided to sell... I really liked working on this one project."

The Aspiring Entrepreneur

"I'm building a business, not just an app." Selective Fit

Demographics

  • Age: 26-40 years old
  • Location: US, Western Europe, tech hubs
  • Senior developer or recently indie
  • Strong technical skills

Their App

  • 20K-200K MAU, productivity/SaaS-like
  • $200-800/month (some monetization)
  • Native or modern frameworks
  • Regular updates (monthly+)

Say YES When

  • Partner is "business co-founder"
  • Learning opportunity included
  • Collaborative decision-making
  • Performance-based terms

Say NO When

  • Treated like a contractor
  • No input on strategy
  • Micromanagement or control loss
  • Long lock-in, no renegotiation

"Success in entrepreneurship is not just about having technical expertise, but also about being able to effectively communicate and promote your product."

The Emerging Market Builder

"My app has users but the geography kills revenue." Good Fit

Demographics

  • Age: 24-32 years old
  • Location: India, Brazil, Eastern Europe
  • Mid-level developer ($20-50K)
  • 4-8 years experience

Their App

  • 30K-200K MAU, education/utilities
  • $100-500/month (ARPU 4x lower)
  • Native Android or Flutter
  • Regular updates, maintained

Say YES When

  • Legitimate business (verified identity)
  • USD payments, transparent terms
  • Respects their creation and users
  • Evidence of international partnerships

Say NO When

  • Vague about Western market access
  • Unclear payment terms
  • No verifiable track record
  • Condescending about origin market

"My app has 100K users but makes $300/month because they're all in India."

Universal Trust Signals

What ALL developers look for before partnering.

Must-Haves

  • Developer retains 100% IP ownership
  • Proven track record with case studies
  • Transparent terms and payment schedules
  • Responsive communication
  • Verifiable business identity

Red Flags (Deal-Breakers)

  • IP ownership transfer of any kind
  • Vague "recoupable costs" clauses
  • High share without minimum guarantee
  • Automatic sequel/DLC rights
  • Pressure to sign quickly

Revenue Share Reality

70/30 is industry standard (developer favor)

50/50 requires substantial justification

Consider: Pilot at 70/30, move to 50/50 after proving value

"You're building a business, not just an app. Partner with us as your business co-founder."
