Ad Mediation Research for Non-Gaming Apps

Comprehensive analysis of ad mediation platforms, eCPM benchmarks, and monetization strategies for Moonshot's indie app partners

Executive Summary

This research analyzes ad mediation options for non-gaming mobile apps (utilities, education, productivity, lifestyle) to inform Moonshot's monetization playbook. Key findings:

Key Insights

  • Platform Recommendation: AdMob is best for indie developers (15K-1M MAU) due to zero fees, ease of use, and excellent fill rates (90%+)
  • eCPM Performance: Interstitial ads outperform banners by 4,094% on average. US interstitial eCPMs peak at $8.57, with rewarded video delivering 3.5x higher retention
  • Non-Gaming Reality: Non-gaming apps see significantly lower eCPMs than gaming apps, but hybrid monetization (ads + subscriptions) can drive strong conversion rates
  • Critical Challenge: Education apps face severe COPPA restrictions (FTC finalized new rules in Jan 2025) that limit ad monetization for apps targeting children under 13

Moonshot's Default Ad Stack Recommendation

Primary Platform: Google AdMob with mediation enabled (no fees, 90%+ fill rate, 25+ network access)

Ad Format Mix:

  • Interstitial ads at natural transition points (highest revenue)
  • Rewarded video for optional premium features (best UX/retention balance)
  • Native ads for content-heavy apps (4-8x higher CTR than banners)
  • Minimize or avoid banner ads (lowest performance, banner blindness)

Hybrid Strategy: Offer "Remove Ads" IAP at $1.99-$2.99 to convert ad-averse users while maintaining free tier with strategic ad placement

3. Ad Placement Best Practices for Non-Gaming Apps

Core Principles

Balancing ad revenue with user experience is critical for long-term retention. Poor ad placement can destroy an app's value proposition.

The UX vs Revenue Tradeoff

Interstitial ads generate 40x more revenue than banners but are "intentionally designed to be intrusive." The key is strategic placement at natural break points where users expect pauses.

Placement Strategies by Ad Format

Ad Format Ideal Placement (Non-Gaming) Frequency Recommendation UX Impact
Interstitial
  • After completing a task/action
  • Between major navigation transitions
  • When saving/exporting work
  • After time-based milestones (e.g., 5 min of use)
 Max 1 every 60-90 seconds of active use High disruption - use sparingly
Rewarded Video
  • Unlock premium feature temporarily
  • Remove ads for 30-60 minutes
  • Get extra storage/exports/credits
  • Access tutorial or advanced content
 User-initiated (no limit) Positive - users choose to engage
Native Ads
  • Within content feeds/lists
  • Between articles/lessons
  • In search results (marked as ads)
  • Related recommendations section
 1 ad per 5-10 content items Low disruption - blends with content
Banner Ads
  • Bottom of screen (avoid top)
  • After primary content area
  • AVOID: Near action buttons
  • AVOID: Covering content
 Persistent (but minimize overall use) Banner blindness - low effectiveness

App-Category-Specific Guidance

Utility Apps

Examples: Calculator, file manager, QR scanner, note-taking

Best Strategy:

  • Interstitial after completing 3-5 actions
  • Rewarded video to unlock premium tools temporarily
  • AVOID persistent banners (kills usability)

Key Metric: Session frequency matters more than length - cap ads per session

Education Apps

Examples: Language learning, flashcards, tutorials, reference

Best Strategy:

  • Rewarded video to unlock next lesson/level
  • Interstitial between lessons (not mid-lesson)
  • Native ads in resource libraries

Critical Warning: COPPA restrictions for apps targeting children under 13 (see section 5)

Productivity Apps

Examples: To-do lists, calendars, time tracking, habit trackers

Best Strategy:

  • Rewarded video for premium analytics/reports
  • Interstitial when switching between major views
  • AVOID interrupting active workflows

Key Metric: Power users hate ads - prioritize subscription conversion

Lifestyle Apps

Examples: Fitness trackers, recipe apps, meditation, journaling

Best Strategy:

  • Native ads in content feeds (recipes, articles)
  • Rewarded video for workout videos or premium content
  • Interstitial between major sections

Key Metric: Content consumption time is high - more ad opportunities

Advanced Optimization Tactics

AdMob Mediation Best Practices

  1. Enable Auto-Optimization: Let AdMob's algorithm prioritize highest-paying networks automatically
  2. Set eCPM Floor: Minimum thresholds ensure fewer, higher-quality ads display
  3. Use Frequency Capping: Limit ad exposure per user per time period (AdMob supports app/unit-level caps)
  4. Segment User Groups: Create separate ad units for new users vs power users vs tier 1 geography
  5. A/B Test Placements: Test different timing/frequency combinations and measure retention impact
  6. Monitor Key Metrics:
    • eCPM trends by format and geography
    • Fill rate (aim for 85%+)
    • Day 1, 7, 30 retention by cohort
    • Revenue per daily active user (ARPDAU)

What NOT to Do

  • Never: Show interstitial during critical user tasks (mid-form, during active editing, while loading important content)
  • Never: Place banners near action buttons (accidental clicks destroy trust)
  • Never: Show ads on first app open (let users understand value first)
  • Never: Auto-play video ads with sound (massive UX violation)
  • Never: Ignore Google's ad policy (account bans are permanent and devastating)

4. Hybrid Monetization: Ads + Subscriptions

The Power of Dual Revenue Streams

Combining ads with subscription/IAP options maximizes revenue by capturing both ad-tolerant users and those willing to pay for ad-free experiences.

Successful Hybrid Models

  • Spotify: Achieved 46% conversion rate from free (ad-supported) to paid users through strategic friction (ads between songs, limited skips)
  • LinkedIn: Tiered freemium model where free users see ads, paid tiers remove ads and unlock features
  • Evernote: Free tier with ads + feature limits, premium removes both restrictions

When to Show Ads vs Hide Them

User Segmentation Strategy

  1. New Users (Day 1-3):
    • Minimal ads initially (let them experience core value)
    • Introduce ads gradually starting Day 2-3
    • Offer premium trial after first value moment
  2. Engaged Free Users (Day 4-30):
    • Full ad load (interstitials, rewarded video)
    • Show "Remove Ads" prompt after frustration signals (skipping ads, rapid clicks)
    • Time-limited discount offers (24-hour flash sales)
  3. Power Users (30+ days):
    • Reduce ad frequency slightly (retention risk)
    • Targeted premium upsells based on usage patterns
    • Personalized "You're a power user" messaging
  4. Premium Subscribers:
    • Zero ads (honor the promise)
    • Upsell to higher tiers if applicable
    • Referral incentives for bringing free users

Revenue Math Example

Scenario: 50K MAU Utility App

Ad-Only Model:

  • 50K MAU × 10 sessions/month × 2 interstitials/session = 1M impressions
  • eCPM: $5 (US tier 1)
  • Monthly Revenue: 1M impressions × $5 / 1000 = $5,000/month

Hybrid Model (Ads + Remove Ads IAP):

  • 47.5K users stay free (95%) → Ad Revenue: $4,750/month
  • 2.5K users convert (5%) at $1.99 → IAP Revenue: $4,975 one-time → $1,658/month (amortized over 3 months)
  • Total Monthly Revenue: $6,408/month (+28% increase)

Hybrid Model (Ads + Subscription at $4.99/month):

  • 48.5K users stay free (97%) → Ad Revenue: $4,850/month
  • 1.5K users convert (3%) at $4.99/month → Subscription Revenue: $7,485/month (recurring!)
  • Total Monthly Revenue: $12,335/month (+147% increase)
  • Plus: Recurring revenue creates compounding growth

Best Practices for Hybrid Monetization

  • Be Transparent: Clearly communicate what premium removes (ads) and adds (features)
  • Offer Trials: 7-14 day free trials significantly increase subscription conversion
  • Use Social Proof: "Join 10,000+ premium members" messaging builds trust
  • Time Offers: Limited-time discounts create urgency (50% off for 24 hours)
  • A/B Test Everything: Price points, messaging, upgrade prompts, trial lengths
  • Respect Users: Never make free tier deliberately bad just to force upgrades (kills trust)

5. Special Considerations for Non-Gaming Apps

Education Apps & COPPA Compliance

Critical Alert: New COPPA Rules (January 2025)

The FTC finalized major changes to the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) in January 2025, significantly limiting companies' ability to monetize children's data.

Key Restrictions for Apps Targeting Children Under 13:

  • Behavioral Advertising Banned: Cannot use personal data for targeted advertising
  • Contextual Ads Only: Ads must be based on content context, not user tracking
  • No Persistent Identifiers: Severely limits ad network tracking capabilities
  • Parental Consent Required: For any data collection beyond basic app functionality
  • Limited Ad Networks: Many major networks don't support COPPA-compliant inventory

Moonshot Strategy for Education Apps

Given COPPA restrictions, education apps targeting children should:

  1. Prioritize Subscription Monetization: Ads are too limited/risky for children's apps
  2. Family Plans: Market to parents, not kids (parent pays for child's access)
  3. School/District Licensing: B2B model avoids individual data collection
  4. If Using Ads: Use COPPA-compliant networks only (AdMob has COPPA support, but eCPMs will be very low)
  5. Age Gating: Apps for 13+ can use standard ad monetization
