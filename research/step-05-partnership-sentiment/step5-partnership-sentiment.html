Hypothesis Verdicts

Final validation status based on developer forum research, case studies, and industry benchmarks.

Validated
H2: Developers Prefer Partnership Over Acquisition

Finding: ~75% of developers prefer ongoing partnerships over outright sale. Nearly all post-sale stories express regret, centered on loss of identity, autonomy, and long-term financial opportunity.

Key drivers: Autonomy (85% of sources), ongoing income > lump sum (75%), identity/purpose preservation (70%), emotional attachment (60%).

"When you have a pile of money, you turn into a dragon. The goal becomes to sit on the pile of money and make sure it doesn't get smaller."

‚Äî Hacker News discussion on selling startups
Context-Dependent
H3: 50/50 Revenue Share is Acceptable

Finding: 50/50 is the absolute ceiling, not the baseline. Industry standard is 70/30 (developer keeps 70%). 50/50 only acceptable with massive value delivery.

Recommendation: Start at 65/35 (developer keeps 65%) with pilot at 70/30. Move to 50/50 only after proving 5x+ revenue improvement.

"Taking 50% of revenue for 'nothing'? That's the ongoing debate in the indie dev community."

Key Insight: 50/50 is the Ceiling, Not the Baseline

Developers view 50/50 as the maximum acceptable split, only justified when partners provide: (1) significant upfront funding, (2) full marketing campaigns ($10K+ budgets), (3) comprehensive monetization implementation, or (4) significant risk absorption. Without these, 50%+ partner takes are seen as "predatory."

Patterns Across Case Studies

Why Developers Regret Selling
  • Loss of control over product direction
  • Earnout stress (2+ years of anxiety)
  • Emotional attachment to "their baby"
  • Lump sum psychology ("dragon mentality")
Why Developers Choose Partnership
  • Access expertise they lack (monetization, marketing)
  • Preserve autonomy and involvement
  • Benefit from long-term success, not just lump sum
  • Market access (international, platform relationships)

Trust Barriers & How to Overcome Them

What prevents developers from partnering, and how Moonshot can address each barrier.

Barriers Identified

  • Fear of loss of control: Partners making unilateral decisions
  • Unclear agreement terms: Vague contracts lead to disputes
  • Value misalignment: Partner prioritizes profit over product quality
  • Revenue share disputes: Opaque accounting breeds mistrust
  • No exit options: Locked into unfavorable arrangements

Moonshot Solutions

  • Formalize role & authority: Clear contracts defining decision rights
  • Transparent reporting: Open books, monthly reports, audit rights
  • Value alignment screening: Only partner with compatible apps
  • Pilot periods: 3-month trial at 70/30 before commitment
  • Easy exit: 60-day notice, no penalties
