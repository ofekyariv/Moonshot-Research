Scenario Assumptions
All scenarios assume a 100K MAU app currently earning $1,000/mo, 50/50 revenue split, and monthly operational costs of $200. The key variables are development cost (team quality) and revenue uplift (monetization execution).

Revenue Share Benchmarks

Industry comparison shows 50/50 split is at the high end of what partners typically accept.

App Stores (Apple/Google) Developer keeps 70%
SaaS Affiliates Developer keeps 70-80%
Moonshot Target (50/50) Developer keeps 50%
Gaming Publishers Developer keeps 30-50%
Revenue Share Consideration
50/50 split requires strong value justification. Self-publishing only costs developers 30% (app store cut). Consider piloting at 60/40 (developer keeps 60%) to reduce friction, then move to 50/50 after proving value.

Strategic Recommendations

Key adjustments to improve unit economics viability.

1. Target Higher-Value Apps
100K+ MAU (not 15K-50K)
$1K-$5K/mo current revenue (more headroom)
iOS-primary (5.4x higher subscription ARPU)
2. Control Development Costs
Use Eastern Europe developers ($40-$70/hr)
Fixed-price contracts with clear scope
Target simple-to-medium complexity apps
3. Validate Before Commitment
Request current analytics access before partnership
Estimate uplift potential with benchmarks
Only proceed if 3x+ uplift appears achievable
4. Consider Bootstrap Model
Reduce fixed costs to $5K-$10K/mo initially
Use revenue to fund team expansion
Reach profitability with 3-5 apps instead of 10+
